---

leaving limbo is not the same as leaving a relationship.

you're not ending something that was defined.  
you're stepping back from something  
that was real but unnamed —  
because the unnamed state has become unsustainable for you.

that's a different kind of leaving.  
and it requires a different understanding  
of what you're actually doing.

---

## what leaving is

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                         │
│  leaving limbo means:                                   │
│                                                         │
│  removing yourself from a dynamic                       │
│  that costs you more than it gives you                  │
│  in its current form.                                   │
│                                                         │
│  it does not mean:                                      │
│  that what you had wasn't real.                         │
│  that you don't care about her.                         │
│  that the connection failed.                            │
│  that you're giving up on her.                          │
│                                                         │
│  it means:                                              │
│  you've decided you can't stay in the in-between        │
│  and remain intact.                                     │
│                                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

## what you'd actually be losing

be honest about this before you decide.

```
  the daily contact
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  she talks to you every day.
  that would stop, or change significantly.
  the rhythm of your days would be different.
  this is a real loss. don't minimize it.

  the closeness
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  what you have with her is deep.
  that kind of closeness doesn't just
  transfer to someone else immediately.
  there will be a period of absence.

  the future you imagined
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  the version of this that becomes what you want.
  leaving means releasing that version —
  at least for now.

  possibly her, in any form
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  leaving limbo doesn't always mean
  you can return to a clean friendship after.
  sometimes it means losing her entirely for a while.
  sometimes permanently.
  that's a real cost.
```

---

## what you'd be gaining

```
  clarity
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  the background noise stops.
  you're no longer in a waiting state.
  your emotional energy has somewhere to go
  that isn't an unresolved situation.

  yourself back
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  the part of you that's been managed,
  held back, monitored —
  that part gets to relax.
  you stop tracking signals.
  you stop calculating.
  you stop waiting for something to happen.

  space for something else
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  not necessarily immediately.
  but eventually, the space exists
  for something that doesn't require you
  to live in uncertainty indefinitely.

  integrity
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  you made your feelings known.
  you waited with patience and care.
  you decided you couldn't wait indefinitely.
  that's not failure. that's self-respect.
```

## how to leave

leaving limbo can happen in different ways  
depending on what you have and what you need.

**the honest conversation**
```
  when to use this:
  when you're close enough that silence would feel wrong.
  when she deserves to know why the dynamic is changing.

  what to say:
  "i need to be honest with you about something.
   what we have means a lot to me —
   more than i can keep carrying in the form it's in.
   i told you what i want. i understand you need time.
   but i've realized i can't keep being in the middle of this
   without it costing me too much.
   i'm not angry. i'm not trying to hurt you.
   i just need some distance to figure out how to be okay."

  what this does:
  it gives her information.
  it doesn't demand a response or a decision.
  it's honest about your limits without being an ultimatum.
  and it leaves the door open without holding it for her.
```

**the gradual withdrawal**
```
  when to use this:
  when you need to protect yourself first
  before you can have the harder conversation.
  or when the conversation has already happened
  and now you need to actually create the distance.

  what it looks like:
  less initiation.
  shorter responses.
  not filling silences.
  letting the daily rhythm slow down naturally.
  giving both of you space to feel the change
  before it's formally named.
```

## what she might do when you leave

```
  she gives you space
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  she understands. she respects it.
  things quiet down.
  this is the most likely outcome.
  and it may be the right one —
  even if it's painful.

  she reaches out
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  something in your leaving shifts something in her.
  she realizes what the loss actually feels like.
  she comes back with more clarity than before.

  this is possible. it happens.
  but don't leave in order to make this happen.
  that's not leaving — that's strategy.
  leave because you need to.
  if she comes back with something real, assess it then.

  nothing changes
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  the distance settles.
  she accepts it.
  the connection fades.

  this is also possible.
  and if it happens — it tells you something
  about what the connection was able to hold.
```

## the fear of leaving

you know what leaving costs.  
and part of you is afraid that the moment you leave,  
she'll decide she was ready.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                         │
│  this fear is real.                                     │
│  it might even be true.                                 │
│                                                         │
│  but consider:                                          │
│                                                         │
│  if she only decides when she feels the loss —          │
│  that tells you something about                         │
│  what it took for her to choose.                        │
│                                                         │
│  you don't want to be chosen because you left.          │
│  you want to be chosen because she decided.             │
│                                                         │
│  those are different things.                            │
│                                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## after you leave

the feelings don't stop.  
they usually get louder first.

```
  the first weeks:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  absence is loud.
  the daily contact you're used to isn't there.
  you'll feel it as loss — because it is loss.
  this is not a sign you made the wrong decision.
  it's a sign that what you had was real.

  what helps:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  don't check her activity.
  don't analyze what she's doing or not doing.
  fill the space — actually fill it, not just distract.
  something for yourself. something that's only yours.

  what not to do:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  don't reach out in a weak moment to check if she misses you.
  don't frame the leaving as temporary to make it easier.
  leave and let it be what it is.
```

---

## leaving is not the end of her

leaving limbo doesn't close the door forever.

```
  sometimes people need to feel the loss
  to know what they were protecting.
  sometimes distance gives someone
  the clarity that closeness couldn't.

  you can leave and leave the door open.
  you don't have to declare it permanent.
  you just have to be honest:
  "i need distance right now.
   what happens after that, i don't know."
```
