# LIMBO — when what you have is real but has no name

![LIMBO banner](banner.png)

---

there is a specific kind of situation that nobody has a good word for.

it is not a relationship.  
it is not just a friendship.  
it is something in between — something that has the weight of both  
without the clarity of either.

you talk every day.  
you know each other deeply.  
you've said things to each other that you don't say to other people.  
you've shown up for each other in real ways.

and somewhere in all of that, one of you started wanting something more defined.

you said it out loud.  
they didn't say no.  
they said: *i'm scared of losing this.*  
they said: *give it time.*  
they said: *if something can be built here, that's okay.*

and now you're living in the space between what exists and what it could be.

that space has a cost.  
this framework is about understanding that cost,  
what's keeping you in it,  
and what to do about it.

---

## what this framework does

LIMBO helps you:

- understand what the situation actually is — and why it has no name
- read her fear clearly, without projecting and without dismissing it
- measure whether things are moving or stuck
- decide — with open eyes — whether to stay or leave
- handle the specific situations that come up in this kind of relationship
- process what the experience is teaching you, regardless of outcome

---

## what this framework does not do

it does not tell you she is wrong for being scared.  
it does not tell you the answer.  
it does not make the decision for you.

what it does is make the decision clearer and more honest —  
so that whatever you choose, you choose it with open eyes.

## where to start

if you're new to this framework — start with `START-HERE.md`.  
it has a situation router that tells you exactly where to go  
based on where you are right now.

if you want to understand the situation before anything else —  
read `understanding/` in order.

if you're ready to decide — go to `paths/`.

if you need a one-screen reference while in the middle of something —  
open `meta/QUICK-REF.md`.
