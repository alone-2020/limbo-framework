you've been in wanting-mode for a long time.

wanting her to choose.  
wanting clarity.  
wanting the situation to resolve.

and when you've been wanting something long enough,  
it gets harder to separate  
what you actually want  
from what you've been waiting for.

this file is about that distinction.  
because the decision — stay or leave —  
should come from what you want,  
not from the relief of ending the uncertainty.

---

## the question beneath the question

the obvious question is: should i stay or leave?

but underneath that is the real question:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                         │
│  do i want her —                                        │
│  or do i want the version of this                       │
│  that becomes what it should be?                        │
│                                                         │
│  those are related. but they're not the same.           │
│                                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

wanting her means you want her specifically —  
her presence, her way of being, her in your life.

wanting the version means you want the resolution —  
the confirmation, the clarity, the label,  
the relationship that the situation has been promising.

both are valid.  
but knowing which one is driving you  
changes what you decide.

## what you've said about what you want

you've told her you want to make things even better.

that's important.

```
  "even better" implies:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  you're not trying to replace what exists.
  you're trying to grow it.
  you see what you have as a foundation —
  not a consolation prize.

  this matters because:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  you're not asking her to become something different.
  you're asking what already exists
  to be acknowledged as what it is.
  that's a different kind of ask.
  less threatening than she may be treating it.
```

## the three honest questions

before you make any decision, answer these.

**1. if she said yes tomorrow — what do you actually want to happen?**

```
  not the fantasy version.
  not the relief of finally knowing.
  what do you want your life with her to look like?

  can you picture it specifically?
  does that picture still feel right?
  or have months of limbo made it harder to access?
```

**2. if she never decides — what do you do?**

```
  not what you should do.
  what would you actually do?

  would you stay indefinitely?
  would you eventually leave?
  is there a point where you'd stop waiting —
  and if so, when?

  answering this honestly is more useful
  than any framework can give you.
```

**3. what would you need to feel okay — regardless of her answer?**

```
  this is the most important question.

  because your okayness can't depend entirely on her decision.
  if you're okay only if she says yes —
  that's a version of yourself that's been outsourced to her.

  what would you need — in yourself, in your life —
  to feel okay even if this doesn't become what you want?
```

## what limbo does to clarity

limbo makes it harder to know what you want  
because it keeps you focused on one thing.

```
  you spend so much energy reading signals,
  assessing movement, managing your expression,
  waiting for her to decide —

  that the question of what you actually want
  becomes buried under the question of
  what she's going to do.
```

---

## separating what you want from what you're used to

after enough time with someone,  
it becomes difficult to tell  
what you want from what you've normalized.

```
  ask yourself:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  if i met her today — knowing everything i know —
  would i want to pursue this?

  or has the depth of what we have
  made it feel essential
  even if what it is isn't what i need?

  not to diminish what you have.
  but to check that what you're holding onto
  is the actual person
  and not just the familiarity of her.
```
