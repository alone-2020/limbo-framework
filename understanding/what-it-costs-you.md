---

limbo is not free.

it feels manageable because the connection is real  
and the day-to-day is good.  
but underneath the good days,  
there is a cost being paid.

this file is about naming that cost honestly —  
not to make you angry,  
but because you can't make a clear decision  
about something you haven't fully accounted for.

---

## the daily cost

every day in limbo, you carry something.

```
  the background uncertainty
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  even on good days — even on great days —
  there is a layer underneath that knows
  nothing is confirmed.
  you can't fully rest in what you have
  because you don't know if it's yours to rest in.

  the self-monitoring
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  you're aware of yourself around her in a way
  that changes how present you can actually be.
  part of you is always watching —
  how she responds, what this interaction means,
  whether things are moving or staying still.

  the managed expression
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  there are things you feel
  that you don't say — because you've already said them,
  and saying them again feels like pressure.
  so you hold them.
  and holding them has weight.
```

---

## the investment cost

you've invested a lot.

```
  time
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  hours on call. daily contact. presence.
  this isn't nothing.
  time given to something unconfirmed
  is time that's at risk.

  emotional energy
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  caring deeply about someone who is uncertain
  about the future requires a specific kind of effort.
  you're holding feelings that don't have a clear home.

  material investment
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  gifts. shared things. the concrete expressions
  of care you've offered freely.
  these are not mistakes.
  but they are investments in something
  that hasn't been confirmed as mutual in the same way.
```

---

## the opportunity cost

this one is rarely talked about but it's real.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                         │
│  while you're in limbo,                                 │
│  your emotional capacity is largely occupied.           │
│                                                         │
│  you're not fully available                             │
│  for other connections.                                 │
│  not because you don't want to be —                     │
│  but because this takes up the space                    │
│  where something else could exist.                      │
│                                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

limbo doesn't just cost you what you spend on it.  
it costs you what you could have built elsewhere  
with that same investment.

---

## the identity cost

this is the most subtle and the most serious.

```
  in limbo, you start to define yourself
  in relation to her uncertainty.
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  your sense of where you stand
  becomes dependent on her signals.
  a good day with her feels like progress.
  a quiet day feels like regression.
  your internal state tracks hers
  in a way that's not healthy long-term.

  you start to shrink
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  not dramatically. gradually.
  you stop expressing certain things.
  you manage certain emotions.
  you make yourself slightly smaller
  to not disturb the equilibrium.

  you lose the ability to accurately assess
  what you actually want
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  because you've been in wanting-mode for so long —
  wanting her to choose, wanting clarity —
  that what you actually want for yourself
  becomes harder to access.
```

## what makes the cost invisible

the cost is hard to see because the good things are real.

```
  she's there.
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  daily. consistently. with warmth and care.
  when something is present, the cost of its
  limitations is easier to ignore.

  the good days are genuinely good.
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  and good days make the cost feel manageable.
  "it's not so bad. look at today."
  but the cost doesn't disappear on good days.
  it accumulates underneath them.

  you've normalized it.
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  after enough time in any situation,
  it starts to feel like the natural state of things.
  the cost becomes background noise.
  you forget what it felt like
  before you were carrying it.
```

## the question to ask honestly

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                         │
│  if nothing changes for the next six months —           │
│  same dynamic, same uncertainty, same connection —      │
│                                                         │
│  would you be okay?                                     │
│                                                         │
│  not "would you survive."                               │
│  would you actually be okay.                            │
│                                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

answer that honestly.  
it will tell you more than anything else in this framework.
