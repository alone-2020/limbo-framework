---

the hardest thing about limbo is that  
it can look the same whether it's moving or not.

good days feel like progress.  
bad days feel like regression.  
but neither might be either.

this file gives you concrete ways  
to assess whether the situation is actually moving  
toward something — or just stable in place.

---

## the difference between movement and stability

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                         │
│  movement:                                              │
│  the dynamic between you is changing over time.         │
│  the conversation about the future is evolving.         │
│  her comfort with the idea of more is increasing.       │
│  she's doing things that suggest she's working          │
│  through her fear, not just living with it.             │
│                                                         │
│  stability:                                             │
│  things are good but unchanged.                         │
│  the same dynamic, the same uncertainty,                │
│  the same conversations.                                │
│  it feels okay but nothing is actually developing.      │
│                                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

stability is comfortable.  
movement is what you actually need.

## signals that it's moving

```
  she brings up the future unprompted
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  not when you raise it — on her own.
  she references "us" in the future tense
  without you having created the opening.
  this means she's thinking about it independently.

  her comfort with closeness is increasing
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  not just maintaining the current level —
  actively moving toward more.
  more vulnerability. more physical presence.
  more willingness to be seen clearly.

  she's doing her own work
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  talking to someone about her fear.
  acknowledging openly that the limbo isn't fair to you.
  showing that she's aware the current state has a cost
  and she's trying to address it.

  the "why it won't work" answers are changing
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  early on, she had reasons why she couldn't move forward.
  are those reasons getting smaller?
  is she dismantling them herself, slowly?
  or are they the same reasons they've always been?

  small escalations are happening
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  she does something slightly more than before.
  says something slightly more direct.
  creates an opportunity that didn't exist before.
  these small movements are real signals.
```

## signals that it's stuck

```
  the conversation about the future never changes
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  every time it comes up, it's the same.
  same fears. same "give it time."
  same place you were six weeks ago.

  she's comfortable but not progressing
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  the dynamic is stable and warm
  but it's the same stable and warm it was months ago.
  nothing is deepening. nothing is evolving.
  it's good but it's frozen.

  her fear doesn't seem to be decreasing
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  you've been consistent. you've been patient.
  you've made it as safe as you can.
  and the fear is the same size it was before.

  she doesn't acknowledge the cost to you
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  she knows this is unresolved.
  does she acknowledge what it costs you to stay in it?
  or does she focus on what she needs
  without addressing what you're carrying?

  time keeps passing without markers
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  no milestones. no "by this point, i'll know."
  just an open-ended wait with no shape.
```

## the honest assessment tool

look at the last 60 days specifically.

```
  [ ]  has she said anything about the future
       that she hadn't said before?

  [ ]  has the emotional closeness deepened
       or just maintained?

  [ ]  has she done anything that suggests
       she's working through her fear?

  [ ]  has she acknowledged what this costs you?

  [ ]  is there a concrete marker she's moving toward —
       something she's named as a point where she'll know?

  [ ]  do you feel like you're in a different place
       than you were two months ago?
```

```
  5–6 yes:   things are moving. stay aware. give more time.
  3–4 yes:   moving slowly. decide if that pace works for you.
  1–2 yes:   mostly stable. not stuck yet, but watch carefully.
  0 yes:     stuck. this is important information.
```

## the trap of good days

the most dangerous thing about a stuck limbo  
is that it has good days.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                         │
│  a great conversation feels like progress.              │
│  a moment of closeness feels like movement.             │
│  a day where everything feels right                     │
│  makes the uncertainty feel less urgent.                │
│                                                         │
│  but good days in a stuck situation                     │
│  are not the same as movement.                          │
│  they're evidence that what you have is real.           │
│  they're not evidence that it's going somewhere.        │
│                                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

assess over months, not moments.  
one good week doesn't change a three-month pattern.

## what to do with this assessment

```
  if it's moving:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  you have real reason to continue.
  stay aware. keep the timeline honest.
  go to paths/if-you-stay.md.

  if it's stuck:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  you have important information.
  this doesn't mean leave immediately.
  but it means the conversation needs to happen again —
  more directly this time.
  go to harder/when-time-becomes-an-excuse.md.

  if you're not sure:
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  run the assessment again in 30 days.
  if the answer is still unclear after that —
  unclear is itself a signal.
```
