---

there is a specific kind of situation  
that nobody has a good word for.

it is not a relationship.  
it is not a friendship.  
it is something in between —  
something that has the weight of both  
without the clarity of either.

you talk every day.  
you know each other deeply.  
you've said things to each other  
that you don't say to other people.  
you've shown up for each other in real ways.

and somewhere in all of that,  
one of you — or both of you —  
started wanting something more defined.

you said it out loud.  
they didn't say no.  
they said: *i'm scared of losing this.*  
they said: *give it time.*  
they said: *if something can be built here, that's okay.*

and now you're living in the space  
between what exists and what it could be.

that space has a cost.  
this framework is about understanding that cost,  
what's keeping you in it,  
and what to do about it.

## the two things this framework will not do

```
  it will not tell you she's wrong for being scared.
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  fear of losing something real is real.
  her fear isn't manipulation.
  it's the same thing you feel —
  just pointed in a different direction.

  it will not tell you the answer.
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  nobody can tell you whether to stay or leave.
  what this framework does is make the decision
  clearer and more honest —
  so that whatever you choose,
  you choose it with open eyes.
```
